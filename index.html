1. The Evolution of Storytelling: From Words to Visuals

Storytelling has always been a cornerstone of human communication. In ancient times, stories were shared orally—passed down through generations by word of mouth, often accompanied by song or performance. As societies advanced, the written word took over, with books, plays, and manuscripts becoming the dominant means of preserving and sharing tales. But with the rise of technology, a new chapter emerged.

In the 20th and 21st centuries, the advent of photography, cinema, and digital media began to shift storytelling from an entirely verbal exercise to one that could be told through images, video, and graphics. Visuals allowed for more immediate, visceral engagement, offering audiences an emotional experience that words alone could not always convey. The growing influence of social media has accelerated this transition, where powerful images and videos often go viral and capture global attention within moments.
